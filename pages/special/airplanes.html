Airplanes

Douglas DC3

The venerable workhorse of the 1930's, World War II and beyond. My dad had a model of an Eastern Airlines DC-3 hanging over the computer I played Flight Simulator on growing up. A friendly pilot at the local airport let me get inside his DC-3 and look around once.

Manufacturer: Douglas Aircraft Company
First Flight: December 17, 1935
Range: 2,540km
Max Speed: 223kn (257mph, 413km/h)
Cruise Speed: 183kn (211mph, 339km/h)
Service ceiling: 23,200ft
Powerplant: 2× Pratt & Whitney R-1830-S1C3G Twin Wasp 14-cyl. air-cooled two row radial piston engine, 1,200 hp (890 kW) each

Boing 737-700

The Boeing 737 family of aircraft is the second-best selling series of commercial airlines of all time. In particular, I'm fond of the 737-700—the smallest big planes I see all the time. It's quite a shapely, good-looking plane. The 737-200 is also a very good-looking plane.

Manufacturer: Boeing Commercial Airplanes
First Flight: February 9, 1997
Range: 5,421km
Max Speed: Mach 0.82
Cruise Speed: 452kn (520 mph, 837km/h)
Service ceiling: 41,000ft
Powerplant: 2× CFM International CFM56-7B26 turbofan engine, 26,300lbf (117kN) thrust each

North American XB-70 Valkyrie

The B-70 was one of the many aircraft meant to replace the B-52. It never entered service and only two examples were ever produced, but I think it's the most striking, brilliant aircraft to have ever flown. It was a monster in size and speed, and no aircraft has rivaled its ambition and scale to this day.

Manufacturer: North American Aviation
First Flight: September 21, 1964
Range: 6,899km
Max Speed: Mach 3.1
Cruise Speed: 1,738kn (2,000mph, 3,219km/h)
Service ceiling: 77,350ft
Powerplant: 6× General Electric YJ93 afterburning turbojet, 19,900lbf (89kN) thrust each dry, 28,000lbf (120kN) with afterburner

Avro CF-105 Arrow

The greatest plane that never was, hands down. Developed throughout the 1950's, the CF-105 was to be the world's most advanced interceptor and the pride of Canada's burgeoning aviation industry. This absolutely gorgeous jet was—and still is—the pride of many aviation enthusiasts in Canada; you can sitll meet people whose parents or grandparents worked on the project and feel so passionately about it. Diefenbaker shot it down and ordered it completely scrapped and erased from history before it could enter service.

Manufacturer: Avro Canada
First Flight: March 25, 1958
Range: 556km
Max Speed: Mach 1.98
Cruise Speed: 1,136kn (1,307mph, 2,1004km/h)
Service ceiling: 53,000ft
Powerplant: 2× Pratt & Whitney J75-P-3 afterburning turbojet engines, 16,500 lbf (73 kN) thrust each dry, 23,500 lbf (105 kN) with afterburner

Consolidated PBY-5A Catalina

The Canso was revised for a multitude of roles from the 1930's and 1940's, serving in over 25 countries across the world throguh the 1980's. It's a very unsightly, good-looking bird. Later variants were amphibians—it would be an awesome plane to own and fly across Canada if I ever had the chance.

Manufacturer: Consolidated Aircraft
First Flight: March 21, 1935
Range: 4,060km (2,520mi, 2,190nmi)
Max Speed: 315km/h (196mph, 176kn)
Cruise Speed: 201km/h (125mph, 109kn)
Service ceiling: 15,800ft
Powerplant: 2× Pratt & Whitney R-1830-92 Twin Wasp 14-cylinder air-cooled radial piston engines, 1,200 hp (890 kW) each

McDonnell CF-101 Voodoo

In the wake of the Arrow's tragic cancellation, we got this sleek, speedy-looking supersonic interceptor. It patrolled the northern skies at the height of the Cold War and intercepted Soviet aircraft. I really hope to see one fly someday, though recent efforts to restore one to airworthy condition have failed. Due to...decisions...the CF-101 is still the predecessor of our current fleet.

Manufacturer: McDonnell Aircraft Corporation
First Flight: September 29, 1954
Range: 4,060km (2,520mi, 2,190nmi)
Max Speed: Mach 1.72
Cruise Speed: Mach 0.8
Service ceiling: 54,800ft
Powerplant: 2× Pratt & Whitney J57-P-55 afterburning turbojets, 11,990 lbf (53.3 kN) thrust each dry, 16,900 lbf (75 kN) with afterburner

Lockheed SR-71A Blackbird

The SR-71 Blackbird is one of the most legendary planes which needs no introduction. Designed by Skunk Works icon Kelly Johnson, this plane smashed records and kicked ass from 1966 to 1999. To this day, it is the fastest ever manned air-breathing flying machine, travelling from New York to London in 1 hour and 54 minutes.

Manufacturer: Lockheed Corporation
First Flight: December 22, 1964
Range: 5,230 (3,250mi, 2,824nmi)
Max Speed: Mach 3.3
Cruise Speed: 3,540 (2,200mph, 1,910kn)
Service ceiling: 85,000ft
Powerplant: 2× Pratt & Whitney J58 (JT11D-20J or JT11D-20K) afterburning turbojets, 25,000 lbf (110 kN) thrust each
