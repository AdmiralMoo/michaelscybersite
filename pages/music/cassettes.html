Cassette Tapes

Photo: MichaelsCybersite.net, 2024

This is my collection of cassette tapes: they're all real scans because it's too difficult to find images of cassette tape covers online. I've been collecting them sporadically since around 2020; Mötley Crüe/Dr. Feelgood and Mötley Crüe/Theatre of Pain were the two first albums I got at a thrift store. Since then, I've collected them sporadically. I don't have many, but I've listened to the ones I do have a crap ton, and I'm quite fond of them. There were a lot of albums I first listened to and learned to love on tape before I got a copy of them on CD. I first discovered Pink Floyd/The Dark Side of The Moon after finding it randomly at a thrift store one day in the fall of 2020—what a find that was!

For the longest time, I listened to these on my Sony Walkman WM-FX38, but that device has unfortunately died. I don't have a tape deck or a personal stereo, so I haven't listend to these in a couple months. I will get one again soon.

I'm not a fan of Vinyl; when I want my analog fix, I go for cassettes. I like the tape noise and the feel of them; I think they're nifty.
