    • This page contains a list of albums which may be considered Grunge or Grunge-Adjacent.

    Daniel House once said: "it’s kind of hard to say that grunge is one thing. You can say it’s heavy, intense, loud and dirty but it’s also melodic. Some of it is heavier than others, some of it is more complicated"—that's to say there's no way to classify Grunge as one thing or another. The name of this subgenre is a blanket term to connect bands who were around at the same time in generally the same place who all influenced and played with eachother.

    I own most of these albums, and the ones I don't own will be marked as such. This list isn't to be considered exhaustive or definitive—it's only stuff I've listened to and am familiar with—but maybe you'll find something you weren't aware of!


    1986

    Skin Yard/Skin Yard

    Skin Yard's first album was released in 1986 and featured a kick-ass lineup consisting of Ben McMillan at the microphone, Jack Endino on guitar, Daniel House on bass and Matt Cameron on dums. This record is like nothing else put out by any other band in the scene, taking influences from King Crimson and Pink Floyd. It's got a more psychedelic and progressive feel to it than the grittier music they put out later on.

    This CD version is a compilation featuring other unreleased songs.
