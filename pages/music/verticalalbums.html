The Typewriter
Font Albums

This is a broader trend that touched many artists throughout the 1990's. It tells the prospective listener that this artist is authentic and their music posesses a raw quality to it. They've got an imperfect aesthetic, which sets them apart from everyone else; they're different from the in-crowd, just like you. It's a very utilitarian, understated, anti-commercial aesthetic that tells you to read the lyrics and feel something: "we're here for the music, not the money".

These albums all share that typewriter font, are generally from the 1990s and usually use muted colours. I've noticed only a couple, but I'm sure there's plenty more out there.
