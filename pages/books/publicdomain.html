    Jane Austen's Persuasion

    December 20th, 1817

    This was Jane Austen's last completed work before her death in 1817; as such, it's her most polished and streamlined work. You can feel that she's a much more mature and complete author, and it makes me feel sad about the idea of what could've come after had she not died so early.

    This book follows Anne Elliot - a woman born into an upper middle-class family of seven-and-twenty years. Eight years prior, she'd fallen in love with Frederick Wentworth, but her family persuaded her against marrying him because he was young and unproven. Wentworth returns as a storied Captain of the Navy and veteran of the Napoleonic wars. Most importantly, he's still single, leaving room for Anne to hope they can get back together.

    I guess you could say that not much happens over the course of the book, but I was enthralled throughout the whole thing. It's a brooding, suspenseful book, filled with tension, doubt and questioning throughout which kept me engaged over the two days I read it. It transports you into this world where people lived so differently than we do now. It was simpler, but at the same time, equally as complex in its own ways. It's so interesting to think that while farmers were living lives not too dissimilar to Thomas Hardy's Far From The Madding Crowd not too far away, there were people like the Elliots going about their business.

    I enjoyed the descriptions of their way of life, and I enjoyed the overall story between Anne and Fredrick. Jane Austen has quickly become one of my favourite authors; I just really enjoy the worlds she builds and the way she writes. She lets you get into the minds of her characters who are put into complex social situations.

    In particular, I also really liked the main character, Anne Elliot. I found her to be very likeable and mature right from the get-go.
